Veterinary Studies

Evidence

Veterinary studies demonstrating the wound-healing properties of Abilar resin ointment in controlled animal models.

Study 1

Piglet Wounds

Controlled Veterinary Study

A controlled veterinary study assessed Abilar efficacy in standardized full-thickness dermal wounds created in 24 piglets (Sus scrofa). The ointment was applied every three days over a 21-day observation period alongside untreated control wounds on the contralateral flank. Wound area, re-epithelialization rate, and histological tissue architecture were evaluated at days 7, 14, and 21.

Key Finding

Re-epithelialization was 52% faster in Abilar-treated wounds vs. untreated controls; improved dermal collagen organization confirmed by histology at day 21.

Study 2

Laser Wounds

Experimental Animal Model

An experimental study using a standardized CO₂ laser-induced wound model in 18 laboratory animals evaluated Abilar's pro-healing mechanisms over a 28-day observation period. Anti-inflammatory cytokine expression, antimicrobial activity, and wound tensile strength were measured at days 7, 14, and 28. Results were compared against both untreated wounds and a standard-of-care ointment group.

Key Finding

Collagen deposition increased by 65% and wound tensile strength improved by 48% vs. standard-care controls at day 28.
